The “Castel Sainte-Cécile” is a house situated in Linkebeek, on the outskirts of Brussels, Belgium. Although “Castel” means “small castle” in French, the house is at most a country house. Cécile is the patron saint of Linkebeek.

The house is linked to Edmond Picard (a Belgian writer mostly known for his racist and antisemitic stances) and - more importantly - Audrey Hepburn.

The house has been a little extended since, but retains most of the original features and the extensive garden.

The Audrey Hepburn Timeline

May 4th 1929: Audrey Ruston (Hepburn is her stage name) was born (48, rue Keyenveld - 1050 Ixelles) to John Ruston and Ella van Heemstra.

1931: The family moves to Linkebeek and they move into the Castel Sainte-Cécile as tenants. Audrey is about 2 years old.

1934: Sources indicate Audrey (5 years old) and her family spend their time between London, the Netherlands and Belgium.

1935: Her father, who had become a Nazi sympathizer, left the family home without a word.

1937 or 1938: Audrey is sent to a boarding school in Elham, Kent.

1938: The Ruston couple divorces.

1939: They go back to the Netherlands.

1991: Audrey Hepburn visits Belgium, and plans are made for her to visit her childhood home. Sadly, her schedule is too tight.

January 20th 1993: Audrey Hepburn passes away in Switzerland.
